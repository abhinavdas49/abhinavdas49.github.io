Engineering Archive • Feb 2, 2026
Aero-Acoustic Optimization and Experimental Validation of a NACA 2415 Toroidal UAV Propeller
This research addresses the dual challenges of aerodynamic inefficiency and high-frequency noise in small Unmanned Aircraft Systems (sUAS). By transitioning from standard open-tipped blades to a toroidal (closed-loop) geometry, the study demonstrates how eliminating blade-tip vortices, the primary source of induced drag and acoustic signatures, produces measurable improvements in both thrust efficiency and noise output. Results were experimentally validated using a custom-built static test fixture and Arduino-based data acquisition system, achieving an 11% thrust increase and 3.5 dB noise reduction at 300g sustained thrust.
